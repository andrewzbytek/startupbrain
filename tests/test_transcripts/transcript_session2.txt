Session 2 — Business Model and Pricing
Date: 2026-02-05
Participants: Alex, Jordan

We locked in the business model today. It will be a per-facility SaaS annual licence. Not per-user, not usage-based — per facility. Each nuclear site is a single contract. This is important because nuclear plant budgets are allocated per facility and procurement approvals happen at the facility level, not at a group level for small operators.

Pricing anchor is set at £50,000 per facility per year. We arrived at this by working backwards: a nuclear compliance manager costs roughly £80,000-£100,000 per year in salary, and our system should replace or dramatically reduce the workload of at least one FTE. £50K represents roughly 50-60% of that cost, making the ROI case straightforward to present.

Our initial target is 10 facilities as paying customers in year one. At £50K per facility, this represents £500,000 ARR. This is enough to sustain us and demonstrate product-market fit to investors.

We also agreed that we will charge a one-time implementation fee of £10,000-£15,000 per facility to cover onboarding, document migration, and initial setup. This is separate from the annual licence and helps us offset the cost of white-glove onboarding for early customers.

The pricing will be reviewed after we close the first three customers. If we can close at £50K without price objections, we will raise the anchor to £75K for subsequent customers. We are deliberately anchoring below what the market will likely bear in order to close early customers quickly and get case studies.

Payment terms will be annual in advance. Monthly billing is not on the table — we need predictable cash flow and annual contracts also reduce churn risk for a compliance product.
